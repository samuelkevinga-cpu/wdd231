NestU

Site Plan Document — Kevin Samuel Pacheco García

Site Identity

Site Name

NestU

Name Rationale

The name combines “Nest” (a safe, comfortable home) with “U” (short for University), directly communicating that this is a student-oriented living space.

Site Purpose

NestU is a responsive three-page website targeting university students seeking affordable co-living and hostel housing. It provides information on budget-friendly room options, study amenities, and community features.

Students can browse rooms dynamically loaded from a JSON file, submit booking requests through a validated form with student email verification and a mock payment gateway, and access location and weather information to help them plan their stay.

Planned Pages

  • Home — Hero, amenities overview, featured rooms, and quick links.
  • Rooms — Room cards loaded from rooms.json, filters, and booking call-to-action.
  • Visit & Book — Map/location, live weather, validated booking form, and mock payment.

User Scenarios

“How much does a shared room cost per month, and what study amenities are included?”

“Is NestU close to my university campus, and what is the weather like in that area right now?”

Color Scheme

Primary greens establish brand identity; light blue accents add contrast for secondary cards, scenario quotes, and wireframe alternates.

  • #1b5e20 — Strong deep green: header background and main section headings.
  • #388e3c — Medium green: links, borders, and accent elements.
  • #e8f5e9 — Light green: paragraph background cards and info boxes.
  • #ffffff — White: main page background.
  • #212121 — Dark gray: all body text for readability.
  • #e3f2fd — Light blue: contrast cards, scenario blocks, and alternate wireframe zones.
  • #0288d1 — Medium blue: hover states, accent borders, and secondary emphasis.

Typography

Poppins (Google Font) — all headings (h1–h3) and the site name.

Inter (Google Font) — all body text, paragraphs, and labels.
